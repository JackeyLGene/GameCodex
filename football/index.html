How we read a match

When in the match. Where on the pitch.

The method starts with one game: Argentina vs France, the 2022 final. The simple read is: when does the play happen, and where is the ball or action on the pitch? Over 120 minutes, those points become a game shape.

Goals are punctures. The score records the break; the shape records the pressure.

Sideline note

Use World Cup drift as a scene, not a verdict.

A team can look different from one World Cup to the next. With only a few matches per team, and the next tournament resetting the cast, the page treats that as a story cue, not a forecast.

The counterintuitive part

Your style is partly the opponent's fingerprint.

Costa Rica invite teams forward. Spain and Brazil push them back. Uruguay barely changes no matter who is across the pitch. Germany bends with the matchup. This is why a team's "style" is never fully its own.

What this is

A portable structural lens, not a football oracle.

Data

StatsBomb Open Data, 2018 and 2022 World Cups. The page is built for complete event datasets after matches are played, not live tournament tracking.

Method

Every event is encoded as time context plus pitch rhythm. Team fingerprints are averages over those event coordinates.

Limits

No xG, no score prediction, no possession-chain model. Read strength marks structural readability, not match outcome probability.
