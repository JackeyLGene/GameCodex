# Fingerprints on the World Cup - Page Plan

## Positioning

Title: Fingerprints on the World Cup

Subtitle: A structural analysis of how teams shape the game

The page is a post-tournament structural lens, not a score predictor, roster
forecast, or live tracker. It shows how teams imposed pitch position, pressure,
event mix, and matchup shape in complete World Cup event data, using the same
SHP logic previously used for Go.

## Design Language

- Palette: quiet pitch green, black, white, pale field-line accents.
- Feel: tactical board, not sports-news dashboard.
- Visual grammar: pitch diagrams, dots, arrows, compact cards, read-strength tags.
- Avoid: xG language, win prediction, team-quality ranking, live-update
  language, and overclaiming from one tournament sample.

## Narrative Flow

1. Hero
   - Direct promise: we do not predict scores or live rosters; we read game
     shape after event data is complete.
   - Show 128 matches, 456,536 located events, 32 teams, 8 featured matchups.

2. How We Read a Match
   - Use Argentina vs France, 2022 final.
   - Each event is a coordinate with a time label.
   - Tabs: 0-45 min, 75-90 min, 90-120 min.
   - Pitch dots and team readouts make SHP intuitive before abstraction.

3. Team Fingerprints
   - Scatter: territory x pressure.
   - Click a team to reveal pass, pressure, shot, territory, distinctiveness.

4. Sideline Drift Note
   - 2018 to 2022 drift is kept as a low-sample scene-setting cue.
   - Claim boundary: World Cup drift can suggest visible movement, but cannot
     prove player, coach, or future-roster effects.

5. Opponent Mirror
   - Opponent-induced x-position shift.
   - Negative delta: this opponent pins teams back.
   - Positive delta: this opponent invites/absorbs forward play.

6. Matchup Explorer
   - Any two 2022 teams.
   - Outputs style distance, territory edge, press edge, pass/control edge,
     volatility note, and read strength.
   - Read strength is structural readability, not win confidence.

7. Notes
   - StatsBomb Open Data attribution.
   - Limitations: two World Cups, event-coordinate reading, not xG, not outcome
     prediction, no possession-chain model, not real-time updating.

## Risk Controls

- Say "shape", "tendency", "post-tournament", and "structural lens"; avoid
  "will win" and live-preview phrasing.
- Display read-strength tags on matchup reads.
- Keep the method visible enough that users understand what a fingerprint is.
- Treat coach/star and future-roster continuity claims as scene-setting only, not causal
  proof.
- Do the stronger version after a tournament ends, when complete event data can
  be encoded.
